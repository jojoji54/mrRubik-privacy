# Biteva Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 9, 2026

## 1. Introduction

Biteva is an application designed to help users keep track of products approaching their expiry date, receive reminders, and get recipe suggestions based on selected ingredients.

This Privacy Policy explains what data the app handles, for what purposes, and how that data is managed.

## 2. Data Controller

Service owner: Biteva

Privacy contact email: `mrrubikdev@gmail.com`

## 3. What Data Is Processed

Biteva may process the following data:

- Products registered by the user
- Product name
- Expiry date
- Category, location, quantity, and notes
- Product images, if the user chooses to add them
- Language, theme, and notification preferences
- Recipe history
- Coin balance and usage related to recipe functionality
- Store purchase identifiers when in-app purchases are made

## 4. Data Stored Locally

Most Biteva functional data is stored locally on the user’s device, including:

- Product inventory
- Expiry dates
- Saved product images
- App settings
- Local recipe history
- Local coin balance and recorded usage

## 5. Device Permissions

Biteva may request the following permissions:

- Camera: to capture product images or scan expiry dates
- Photos or gallery: to attach existing images
- Notifications: to send local reminders before a product expires

The app only uses these permissions when the user enables or triggers the related functionality.

## 6. Recipes and External Services

When the user requests recipes, Biteva may send selected ingredient names and the context required to generate a response to an external recipe-generation service.

This may include:

- Names of selected ingredients
- Current app language
- Time of generation
- Whether the user requested an image for the recipe

Product photos are not sent to generate recipes unless a future version explicitly states otherwise.

## 7. Remote Configuration

In the current test version, Biteva uses Firebase Firestore to read remote configuration required for the recipe integration.

That configuration does not contain personal user information.

## 8. In-App Purchases

If the user purchases coins inside the app, the transaction is processed through Apple App Store or Google Play, depending on the platform.

Biteva does not access the user’s full payment card details. Financial information is handled by the relevant platform provider.

## 9. Purpose of Processing

Data is used to:

- Manage inventory and expiry dates
- Display product states and reminders
- Generate recipes from selected ingredients
- Maintain recipe history
- Manage coin balance and purchases
- Improve app stability and functionality

## 10. Legal Basis

The legal basis for processing is:

- Performance of the service requested by the user
- User consent when granting permissions such as camera, photos, or notifications
- Legitimate interest in keeping the app operational and secure

## 11. Data Retention

Locally stored data remains on the user’s device until the user:

- Deletes it manually
- Uninstalls the app
- Resets app data

Data sent to external service providers is governed by the policies of those providers and by the technical configuration active at the relevant time.

## 12. Data Sharing with Third Parties

Biteva does not sell personal data.

The app may rely on third parties only to provide specific functionality, such as:

- Firebase
- App Store / Google Play
- The external recipe-generation provider

## 13. Security

Biteva is designed to minimize unnecessary processing and prioritizes local storage for core inventory data. However, no system can guarantee absolute security.

## 14. User Rights

Users may:

- Delete products and app data from within the app
- Revoke system permissions from the device settings
- Request additional information by contacting support or the privacy contact

## 15. Children

Biteva is not specifically designed for children and is not intentionally directed to them.

## 16. Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated to reflect legal, technical, or product changes. Users should review the latest version periodically.
